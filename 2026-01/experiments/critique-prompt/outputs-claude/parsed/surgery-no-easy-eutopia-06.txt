**Load-bearing claim:**
Joint-aggregation bounded views are implausible due to "scale tipping"

**Attack type:**
Parameter sensitivity

The paper argues that jointly-aggregating bounded views permit absurd "scale tipping" where tiny changes to cosmic-scale goods/bads balance can flip the future from extinction-level to eutopian. But this critique assumes the bounded function is approximately linear near zeroâ€”that is, small net differences in goods minus bads translate to proportionally small differences in value. If instead the bounded function has a steep gradient near zero but flattens at extremes (like a hyperbolic tangent centered at zero), then a civilization-sized shift from net-negative to net-positive represents a genuine moral achievement, not an arbitrary tipping point. The "scale tipping" intuition pump conflates the mathematical property of bounded functions with a specific functional form assumption the paper never defends. If this critique fails, the paper's main route to an easygoing view reopens.
